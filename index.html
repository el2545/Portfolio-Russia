Core research thesis

My research direction is to build decision-support models that connect physical flows, financial performance, urban infrastructure, and strategic governance. I want to develop this path in a Russian university, where science is not separated from a national school of thought, engineering seriousness, and responsibility toward the future.

  • In the spirit of Lomonosov: approach science as a matter of dignity, labor, and intellectual maturity.
  • In the logic of Mendeleev: seek order in complex systems, classify variables, and transform complexity into method.
  • In the horizon of Gagarin: see technological progress as a courageous, collective, and disciplined movement forward.
Method Measurement, verification, reproducibility.
Tool Models, dashboards, simulations, scenarios.
Contribution Outputs that serve the shared academic work.

Project 01 · Casablanca Exposure Model

My Casablanca outdoor-advertising project showed that urban visibility can be measured, modelled, and translated into investment logic. It is not only a local case; it is an early form of a method that can be extended toward smart-infrastructure analysis.

  • SUMO FCD outputs and QGIS spatial reasoning.
  • ROI, CAPEX, OPEX, TCO, and scenario comparison.
  • Shift from urban intuition to verifiable analytics.
